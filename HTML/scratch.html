The History of College Football

College football began in the late 19th century, with the first official game played between Rutgers and Princeton on November 6, 1869. Over time, the sport grew in popularity, becoming a cultural phenomenon in the United States.

In the 20th century, college football established itself as a powerhouse in American sports, with legendary teams like Notre Dame, Alabama, and Michigan setting records and drawing large crowds.

The Evolution of the College Football Playoffs

The college football playoff system has evolved over the years to provide a more structured way of determining the national champion. Before the playoff era, national champions were chosen through polls like the AP and Coaches' Poll, often leading to controversy and split titles. In 1998, the Bowl Championship Series (BCS) was introduced, using rankings to select the top two teams for a championship game.

However, the BCS still faced criticism for leaving out deserving teams. In 2014, the College Football Playoff (CFP) was established, allowing the top four teams to compete in a playoff format. The CFP has brought more excitement and fairness to the postseason. In 2024, the system will expand to 12 teams, giving more teams the opportunity to compete for the national title.
